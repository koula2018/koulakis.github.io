Mathematics

I have been a big fun of mathematics from a young age. Up to now I have followed two major paths in this field, one in mathematical logic and one in discrete mathematics and the applications of probability theory in computer science. I stepped into the "logic" path during the last years of highschool and finished its (first?) round with my PhD in set theory. I am still exploring the second path, which is a bit more informal and started during my masters in Athens. Currently I am interested in the applications of probability theory and statistics in data mining as well as the theoretical representations of distributed processes.

Set Theory

Set theory has always been fascinating to me because it is the most natural example of a mathematical theory whose main questions are not provable using the traditional foundations of mathematics. A large portion of those questions are attempting to clarify the nature of the real numbers, for example trying to determine which sets of reals we can assign probabilities to. Interestingly, most of those questions can be categorized by using a sequence of different levels of infinity called large cardinals. This has been the topic of my master thesis, supervized by Athanassios Tzouvaras, which is actually a short survey presenting the core properties of several "popular" large cardinals. Later on, I got to work with Ralf Schindler, my PhD suprvisor, on a problem relating a category of large cardinals with a set of properties of the real numbers.
